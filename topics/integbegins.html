Topic 4- Integration begins

1. Primitive function's introduction

This lesson will introduce the concept of the primitive function or antiderivative.

Syllabus dot-points covered:

-Define anti-differentiation as the reverse of differentiation and use the notation ∫ 𝑓(𝑥) 𝑑𝑥 for antiderivatives or indefinite integrals.

-recognise that any two anti-derivatives of 𝑓(𝑥) differ by a constant.

2. Indefinite integrals

We will learn about indefinite integrals, their properties, and techniques for evaluating them.

Syllabus dot-points covered:

-Define anti-differentiation as the reverse of differentiation and use the notation ∫ 𝑓(𝑥) 𝑑𝑥 for antiderivatives or indefinite integrals.

-recognise that any two anti-derivatives of 𝑓(𝑥) differ by a constant.

-establish and use the formula ∫ 𝑥^𝑛𝑑𝑥 = 1 /𝑛+1 𝑥^(𝑛+1) + 𝑐, for 𝑛 ≠ −1 .

-establish and use the formula ∫ 𝑓′(𝑥)[𝑓(𝑥)]^𝑛𝑑𝑥 = 1/𝑛+1 [𝑓(𝑥)] ^𝑛+1 + 𝑐 where 𝑛 ≠ −1 (the reverse chain rule).

-determine indefinite integrals of the form ∫ 𝑓(𝑎𝑥 + 𝑏) 𝑑𝑥 .

-determine 𝑓(𝑥), given 𝑓′(𝑥) and an initial condition 𝑓(𝑎) = 𝑏 in a range of practical and abstract applications including coordinate geometry, business and science.

3. Primitives of trigonometric functions

We will cover the primitives of trigonometric functions, including integral identities and their uses.

Syllabus dot-points covered:

-establish and use the formulae for the anti-derivatives of sin (𝑎𝑥 + 𝑏), cos (𝑎𝑥 + 𝑏) and sec^2(𝑎𝑥 + 𝑏) .

4. Integrating the exponential function

The process of integrating the exponential function will be discussed in detail.

Syllabus dot-points covered:

-establish and use the formulae ∫ 𝑒^𝑥𝑑𝑥 = 𝑒^𝑥 + 𝑐 and ∫ 𝑒^𝑎𝑥+𝑏𝑑𝑥 = 1/𝑎 𝑒^𝑎𝑥+𝑏 + c .

-establish and use the formulae ∫ 𝑎^𝑥𝑑𝑥 = 𝑎^𝑥/ln 𝑎 + C .

5. Integrating the hyperbolic function

We will dive into the integration of logarithmic functions, explaining the method and its implications.

Syllabus dot-points covered:

-establish and use the formulae ∫ 1/𝑥 𝑑𝑥 = ln |𝑥| + 𝑐 and ∫ 𝑓 ′ (𝑥) 𝑓(𝑥) 𝑑𝑥 = ln |𝑓(𝑥)| + 𝑐 for 𝑥 ≠ 0, 𝑓(𝑥) ≠ 0, respectively.
